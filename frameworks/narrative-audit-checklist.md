# Narrative Audit Checklist

How to diagnose whether your GTM org actually shares a narrative or just thinks it does. Forrester found that 82% of executives believe their teams are aligned while 65% of practitioners say they aren't. This checklist helps you find the truth.

---

## The Test

Ask five people from five different functions the same question: **"Why should a customer choose us right now?"**

Talk to one person from each:
- [ ] Sales
- [ ] Product
- [ ] Marketing
- [ ] Customer Success
- [ ] Executive team

Don't prep them. Don't give them context. Just ask.

---

## What to listen for

### Strong narrative alignment looks like:

- [ ] All five mention the same market shift or customer problem
- [ ] All five describe a similar customer outcome (not feature list)
- [ ] The language is close but not identical (internalized, not memorized)
- [ ] Nobody needs to reference a document to answer
- [ ] The answers would make sense to a prospect who's never heard of you

### Weak narrative alignment looks like:

- [ ] Sales leads with product features or competitive comparisons
- [ ] Product describes the roadmap instead of the customer problem
- [ ] Marketing recites taglines or campaign themes
- [ ] CS talks about retention mechanics instead of customer value
- [ ] Executives give a different answer than everyone else
- [ ] Multiple people say "it depends" or "we're still figuring that out"

---

## Scoring

**5/5 aligned:** Rare. Your narrative is working. Refresh it quarterly to keep it current.

**3-4/5 aligned:** Common in good orgs. Identify which function is drifting and why. Usually it's because they weren't part of building the narrative.

**1-2/5 aligned:** You don't have a shared narrative. You have a messaging doc that one team wrote and everyone else ignored. Start from scratch with the Shared Narrative Canvas.

**0/5 aligned:** More common than anyone admits. This is your highest-leverage project right now.

---

## Deeper diagnostics

If you want to go further, ask these follow-up questions:

### To Sales:
- "What's the first thing you say in a discovery call?"
- "What objection do you hear most often?"
- If their opening doesn't connect to the narrative, the narrative isn't reaching them.

### To Product:
- "How do you decide what to build next?"
- "What customer problem keeps coming up?"
- If their prioritization framework doesn't reference the same market shift, product and marketing are solving different problems.

### To Customer Success:
- "Why do customers stay?"
- "Why do they leave?"
- If their retention story doesn't match the acquisition narrative, you have a promise gap.

### To the Executive team:
- "If you had 30 seconds to explain what we do, what would you say?"
- If the CEO's answer doesn't match the sales team's answer, nobody else's will either.

---

## What to do with the results

Don't turn this into a PowerPoint. Don't present findings in an all-hands. Do this:

1. Write down the gaps you found.
2. Build (or rebuild) the Shared Narrative Canvas.
3. Test the new narrative with the same five people.
4. Repeat in 90 days.
