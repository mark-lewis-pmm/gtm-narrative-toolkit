# GTM Narrative Toolkit

Practical frameworks for building shared narratives that align go-to-market organizations. Built for product marketing leaders, but useful for anyone responsible for cross-functional GTM execution.

## Why this exists

Most GTM orgs treat alignment as a coordination problem. More meetings, more syncs, more status updates. But the real issue is usually simpler: nobody shares the same story about what you're building, who it's for, or why it matters right now.

These frameworks help you build that shared story and operationalize it across sales, marketing, product, and customer success.

## What's inside

### /frameworks

- **shared-narrative-canvas.md** — A one-page template for building your company's shared narrative. Five sections, five questions. Clear enough that anyone in the org can retell it from memory.
- **narrative-audit-checklist.md** — How to diagnose whether your GTM org actually shares a narrative or just thinks it does.
- **quarterly-narrative-refresh.md** — A lightweight process for pressure-testing and updating your narrative every 90 days.

### /templates

- **stakeholder-alignment-map.md** — Map your key stakeholders, what they care about, and how the narrative connects to each of them.
- **launch-narrative-brief.md** — A pre-launch document that separates the narrative (the story) from the logistics (the plan).

### /references

- **reading-list.md** — Curated articles, research, and case studies on narrative-driven alignment. Sources include HBR, McKinsey, Forrester, Gartner, and practitioners like Andy Raskin, April Dunford, and Richard King.

## How to use this

Pick the framework that matches your situation. Adapt it. Don't treat these as fill-in-the-blank templates. They're thinking tools.

If you're a PMM leader joining a new company, start with the **shared-narrative-canvas** and the **narrative-audit-checklist**.

If you're mid-flight and things feel misaligned, start with the **quarterly-narrative-refresh**.
